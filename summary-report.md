\# Summary Report



\## Summary

This project reviews the Amazon online store experience from a visual QA and UX perspective. The analysis focuses on how users search for products, review product details, and interact with key shopping information.



\## Main Focus

The purpose of this project is to identify friction points, visual clarity issues, usability concerns, and possible interface improvement opportunities that may affect the shopping journey.



\## Findings Documented

\### Finding 01 – Product page presents excessive information density

The product detail page includes too much information competing for user attention at the same time, making it harder to quickly identify the most relevant purchase details.



\### Finding 02 – Product title is too long and reduces scanability

The title contains too many product attributes in one block, which reduces clarity and makes first-glance understanding more difficult.



\### Finding 03 – Key product benefits are not easy to scan quickly

The “About this item” section contains long descriptive bullet points, making the product benefits harder to scan efficiently.



\## Review Approach

The review is based on real navigation scenarios and screenshot evidence. Each finding is documented with attention to user impact, scanability, clarity, and possible improvement opportunities.



\## Improvement Direction

The main improvement opportunities identified in this analysis focus on:

\- Better information hierarchy

\- More scannable content presentation

\- Clearer product titles

\- Faster recognition of key benefits



\## Conclusion

This project works as a practical and visual showcase of QA and UX analysis skills applied to a real online store experience.
